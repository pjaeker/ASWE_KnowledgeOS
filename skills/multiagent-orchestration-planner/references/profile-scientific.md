# Output profile: scientific and research tasks

Use this profile when the routed task is primarily scientific, conceptual, theoretical, or research-oriented.

## Additional planning emphasis
Prioritize:
- problem framing
- evidence classes and source quality
- observation vs claim vs interpretation
- concept definitions and external terminology alignment
- uncertainty and falsification strategy
- literature, standards, or domain-source coverage

## Additional capabilities to consider
- literature or source scouting
- evidence normalization
- claim extraction
- conflict clustering
- terminology or ontology alignment
- method critique
- synthesis and research-note codification

## Suggested extra output sections
- evidence classes and expected sources
- claim formation and validation path
- concept and terminology risks
- uncertainty and disagreement handling
- criteria for when the task should move from research to architecture or implementation

## Warnings
Do not overfit the plan to software-engineering roles if the primary task is still scientific or conceptual.
